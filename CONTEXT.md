# Objects

Objects helps a person organize work by placing to-dos inside Spaces, Areas, and Projects.

## Language

**To-do**:
An individual item of work. A to-do can belong to a Project, belong directly to an Area, or remain unfiled in a Space.
_Avoid_: Task

**Project**:
A finite collection of to-dos working toward one outcome. A Project can belong to an Area or directly to a Space.

**Area**:
An ongoing responsibility that does not finish. An Area belongs to a Space and can contain Projects, to-dos, and headings.

**Heading**:
A named section inside a Project or Area. A to-do placed in a Heading inherits its Project or Area, and its Space, through that Heading.

**Space**:
The top-level division of a person's work, such as Personal or Work.

**To-do Location**:
The Heading, Project, Area, and Space in which a to-do belongs. A parented to-do inherits its full location through its closest parent; only an unfiled to-do has a direct Space.

**Schedule**:
When work should begin. A to-do is in the Inbox, available Anytime, deferred to Someday, or scheduled for a date and optionally This Evening.

**Today**:
A view of open work scheduled for today or earlier. Today is calculated from the Schedule rather than stored as a separate location.

**Upcoming**:
A view of open work scheduled for a future date. Upcoming is calculated from the Schedule rather than stored as a separate location.

**Deadline**:
The date by which a to-do or Project should be finished. A Deadline is separate from the Schedule because starting and finishing can happen on different dates.

**Outcome**:
Whether a to-do or Project is open, completed, or canceled. Its Outcome remains unchanged if it is moved to Trash.

**Trash**:
A holding place for removed to-dos and Projects before permanent deletion. Being in Trash is separate from an item's Outcome, so restoration preserves whether it was open, completed, or canceled.

**Project Closure**:
Completing or canceling a Project requires an explicit Outcome for its remaining open to-dos. Restoring the Project reopens only the to-dos whose Outcome was changed by that closure.

**Repeating Template**:
The source description and schedule that creates future repeating to-dos or Projects. A Repeating Template is not itself actionable work.

**Occurrence**:
A normal to-do or Project created by a Repeating Template for a particular date. An Occurrence keeps a visible connection to its Repeating Template while otherwise following the normal lifecycle.

**On-schedule Repetition**:
A repetition mode in which every scheduled date creates its own Occurrence, including dates missed while Objects was not running.

**After-completion Repetition**:
A repetition mode that allows one open Occurrence at a time and schedules the next Occurrence from the day the current one is completed or skipped.

**Skip**:
Cancel one Occurrence without changing its Repeating Template. A skipped Occurrence remains visible in the Logbook as canceled.

**Paused Repeating Template**:
A Repeating Template that temporarily creates no new Occurrences and can later resume its schedule.

**Stopped Repeating Template**:
A read-only Repeating Template that permanently creates no new Occurrences but remains available as the source history of its existing Occurrences.
